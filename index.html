We've all been there: a crucial video call that freezes, a game that lags at the final moment, or a song that buffers during your morning run. It feels random—but it doesn't have to be.

At [Your Mobile Operator], we use advanced network technology called QoS (Quality of Service) and QCI (QoS Class Identifier) to actively manage data traffic. Think of it as a smart, multi-lane superhighway for your data, ensuring the most important packets always get the green light.

The Data Superhighway Analogy

Imagine your mobile data is a single-lane road. When it's busy, everything—from an ambulance to a grocery truck—gets stuck in the same jam. This is a basic network without QoS.

Now imagine a smart superhighway with dedicated lanes:

  • The Priority Lane: for voice and critical video calls
  • The Express Lane: for real-time gaming
  • The Standard Lane: for browsing & social media
  • The Background Lane: for large updates and downloads

QCI assigns each traffic type to its correct lane. QoS enforces the rules—making sure a “truck” never blocks an “ambulance.”

What is QCI?

QoS Class Identifier (QCI) ensures proper Quality of Service for different types of traffic in LTE. Each QCI defines how the network allocates resources.
